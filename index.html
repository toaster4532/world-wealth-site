Understanding Global Wealth

Global wealth represents the total net worth of all individuals, including financial assets and real estate, minus their debts. The distribution of this wealth reveals significant disparities between regions and population segments.

Key factors influencing wealth distribution include:

  • Economic policies and governance
  • Access to education and opportunities
  • Historical economic development
  • Market conditions and investment opportunities

Wealth Distribution by Percentile

The global wealth pyramid shows striking inequality, with the top 1% holding more wealth than the bottom 90% combined.

Wealth Concentration

The concentration of wealth at the top has been increasing over the past decades. In 2023:

  • Top 1%: Owns 38.2% of global wealth
  • Next 9%: Controls 44.3% of wealth
  • Middle 40%: Holds 16.5% of wealth
  • Bottom 50%: Possesses just 1% of global wealth
